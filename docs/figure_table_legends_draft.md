# Draft Figure Legends and Table Footnotes

Working title: Sex-equalized high-BMI exposure and potentially avoidable MASLD/NAFLD-related liver burden in Asia and Oceania: a GBD 2023 counterfactual analysis

## Main Figures

**Figure 1. Geographic distribution and country-level ranking of potentially avoidable MASLD/NAFLD-related DALYs under the sex-equalized high-BMI counterfactual, 2023.** Panel A shows country-level potentially avoidable disability-adjusted life-years (DALYs) under Scenario A among adults aged 20 years or older across 34 countries and territories in Southeast Asia, East Asia, and Oceania. Map colour indicates the potentially avoidable DALY rate per 100,000 adults aged 20 years or older, and circle size indicates the absolute number of potentially avoidable DALYs. The colour scale is capped at the 92nd percentile, with overflow indicated. Panel B ranks the ten countries or territories with the largest absolute numbers of potentially avoidable DALYs in 2023. Estimates were derived from Global Burden of Diseases, Injuries, and Risk Factors Study (GBD) 2023 summary outputs and the GBD 2023 high-BMI exposure package using a calibrated exposure-scaling counterfactual anchored to observed high-BMI-attributable burden. Scenario A denotes sex-equalized high-BMI exposure, in which the higher-BMI sex in each location-year-age cell is assigned the high-BMI exposure distribution of the lower-BMI sex. The basemap uses Natural Earth admin-0 country geometries; point locations use study-specific coordinates for all 34 countries and territories. BMI = body-mass index; DALY = disability-adjusted life-year; GBD = Global Burden of Diseases, Injuries, and Risk Factors Study; MASLD = metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease; NAFLD = non-alcoholic fatty liver disease.

**Figure 2. SDI context and non-additive comparison of high BMI, high fasting plasma glucose, and smoking for MASLD/NAFLD-related DALY burden, 2023.** Panel A shows the descriptive country-level association between official Socio-demographic Index (SDI) in 2023 and high-BMI-attributable MASLD/NAFLD-related DALY rates across the study region. Panel B compares high BMI, high fasting plasma glucose (high FPG), and smoking using internally standardized DALY rates per 100,000 adults aged 30 years or older for countries and territories with the highest high-BMI-attributable rates. Estimates were derived from GBD 2023 attributable burden outputs for high BMI, high FPG, and smoking, and the analysis covers 34 countries and territories in Southeast Asia, East Asia, and Oceania. Risk-factor estimates are non-additive and non-mutually exclusive, so values should not be summed across risk factors. The internally standardized 30+ rates were calculated within this analysis to support cross-risk comparison and are not official GBD age-standardized rates. BMI = body-mass index; DALY = disability-adjusted life-year; FPG = fasting plasma glucose; GBD = Global Burden of Diseases, Injuries, and Risk Factors Study; MASLD = metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease; NAFLD = non-alcoholic fatty liver disease; SDI = Socio-demographic Index.

**Figure 3. Internal BAPC scenario projection of MASLD/NAFLD-related burden rates to 2050.** Panel A shows deaths and Panel B shows DALYs for MASLD/NAFLD-related cirrhosis and liver cancer burden across 34 countries and territories in Southeast Asia, East Asia, and Oceania. The black line shows the observed model fit for 1990-2023, the dashed grey line shows the baseline internal Bayesian age-period-cohort (BAPC) projection for 2024-2050, and the blue and orange lines show Scenario A and Scenario B, respectively. Historical burden estimates were based on GBD 2023 summary outputs. Future person-years for 2024-2050 were taken from the United Nations World Population Prospects 2024 PopulationExposureByAge5GroupSex_Medium medium-variant file and mapped to the study age groups. Rates are internal age-standardized rates per 100,000 adults aged 20 years or older, using the study's internal 2023 adult population structure, and are not official GBD age-standardized rates. Scenario A denotes the sex-equalized high-BMI counterfactual, and Scenario B denotes the high-BMI-at-theoretical-minimum-risk-exposure-level (TMREL) theoretical upper-bound comparator. Scenario A and Scenario B are nested deterministic scenario layers and are not additive. The projection is an internal BAPC scenario projection and is not an official Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation (IHME) forecast. BAPC = Bayesian age-period-cohort; BMI = body-mass index; DALY = disability-adjusted life-year; GBD = Global Burden of Diseases, Injuries, and Risk Factors Study; IHME = Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation; MASLD = metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease; NAFLD = non-alcoholic fatty liver disease; TMREL = theoretical minimum risk exposure level; WPP = World Population Prospects.

## Main Tables
